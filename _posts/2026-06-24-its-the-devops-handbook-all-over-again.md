Back in 2018 when I just started my new job,
my new manager took the team through the exercise of reading
[The DevOps Handbook]().[^1]

Me being the precocious 28-year-old I was had actually already read it once before,
but was happy to do it again as a team exercise,
because the lessons contained within those pages were quite important, I felt.

Fast-forward to 2026 and I just finished reading a new book by Gene Kim and Steve Yegge,
Vibe Coding, which much to the surprise of some who still take that term at face value,
at a certain point you realize is actually a quite serious engineering book
that bottles up many of the practices of the DevOps Handbook,
especially with regards to establishing fast feedback loops.

The difference now in 2026 is that we are moving at a million miles and hour
and you are several orders of magnitude more likely to "crash the car" than you were in 2018,
and it underscores the very real need to reinforce these lessons.

I've written before about this phenomenon (Pinnochio is not a real Boy)

I'm no stranger to vibe coding,
but it was comforting reading this book and reinforcing that I wasn't alone (and I wasn't crazy).

[^1] And it wasn't even the second edition, which I just realized came out in 2021 and I'm sure is a great update.
